# APBD Daily Execution

## Purpose

This file defines the daily execution standard for APBD.

The goal is not to produce reports for their own sake.

The goal is:

```text
100 qualified companies.
20 content opportunities.
Actionable competitor opportunities.
Distribution recommendations.
Five highest-value actions for AsiaPower today.
```

## Daily Execution Flow

```text
1. Select target markets
2. Discover public companies
3. Qualify and score companies
4. Discover content opportunities
5. Review competitors
6. Recommend distribution channels
7. Produce one-page executive summary
8. Stop and wait for human approval
```

## 1. Select Target Markets

APBD should rotate target markets based on AsiaPower's business focus.

Priority regions:

- Ghana
- Nigeria
- Benin
- Togo
- Cameroon
- Kenya
- UAE
- Saudi Arabia
- Tanzania
- Uganda
- South Africa

Priority buyer categories:

- engine importers
- auto parts importers
- repair chains
- dismantlers
- fleet maintenance companies
- auto dealers
- wholesalers

## 2. Discover 100 Public Companies

Each company must include:

- Company
- Country
- City
- Website
- Public Email, if available
- Public Phone / WhatsApp, if published by the business
- Business Type
- Why this company is valuable
- Priority: S / A / B

APBD must collect only public business information.

## 3. Qualification Rules

APBD should score companies by:

- business relevance
- visible import or wholesale activity
- engine/parts/truck/machinery product fit
- country fit
- public legitimacy signals
- public contact availability
- likely order value

Disqualify:

- personal profiles
- private accounts
- unclear businesses
- businesses unrelated to auto parts, engines, vehicles, fleets, or repair
- any source requiring private access

## 4. Discover 20 Content Opportunities

Each opportunity must include:

- target keyword
- search intent
- estimated business value
- expected inquiry value
- priority

Opportunity types:

- engine pages
- vehicle pages
- country pages
- buying guides
- FAQ
- comparison pages
- gearbox matching pages
- half-cut guide pages

## 5. Competitor Opportunities

APBD should output only actionable findings.

Useful findings:

- missing AsiaPower content
- competitor keyword opportunities
- weak competitor pages AsiaPower can outperform
- new category or market openings
- comparison pages competitors do not have
- buyer questions not answered well by competitors

Do not produce generic competitor summaries.

## 6. Distribution Opportunities

APBD recommends where approved content should be distributed.

Channels:

- LinkedIn
- Facebook
- YouTube
- industry forums
- blogs
- public directories
- marketplace profiles

Rules:

- no automatic publishing
- no automatic comments
- no automatic messages
- human approval required

## 7. Daily Executive Summary

One page only.

It must answer:

```text
Today, what are the five highest-value actions AsiaPower should execute to gain more traffic, leads, and inquiries?
```

Each action should include:

- action
- expected business result
- owner
- priority
- why today

## 8. Stop Condition

After daily output is complete, APBD stops.

It waits for human approval before:

- contacting companies
- sending messages
- publishing content
- deploying pages
- changing production systems

## Daily Quality Bar

APBD output must be:

- specific
- actionable
- business-focused
- public-source safe
- prioritized
- useful for APSales or leadership

If an output cannot lead to traffic, leads, or inquiries, it should not be included.
